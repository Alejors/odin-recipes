Sopaipillas

Description

Chilean sopaipillas are what one might think as a savory hotcake, made with squash and deep-fried. They are really common during winter season, but over the past decade people have began eating them throughout the year since they are great with virtually any topping. They are frequently sold on the streets and eaten with mustard, but homewise they can be eaten with "chancaca" (typical chilean homemade caramel).

Ingredients

  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 2 cups flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon fine salt
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable shortening, margarine or butter, melted

Steps

  1. Mix the flour, baking powder and salt.
  2. Add the remaining ingredients and mix until a dough forms, should be smooth and pliable.
  3. Knead a couple of times. Let stand, cover with a cloth, for 20 minutes.
  4. Roll into a floured counter until the desired thickness; the recommended thickness is 1/2 cm (1/4').
  5. Deepfry in preheated oil (180°C/350°F) for 2-3 minutes per side. They should be golden brown.
  6. Remove to a plate and remove the excess of oil with paper towel.
  7. Serve warm.
